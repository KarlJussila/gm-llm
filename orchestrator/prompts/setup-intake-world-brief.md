A new campaign is being created. Load `campaign-setup` for the authoring standards and principles that apply throughout init. Then load `campaign-intake` and follow its task list to gather the campaign brief from the player. When intake is complete, load `world-build` and `canon-conventions` and follow their task lists to build the world skeleton and write `campaign/campaign.md`.

This stage is an interactive conversation — keep working with the player until intake and the world skeleton are genuinely done. When they are, **your closing message presents a spoiler-free overview of the world they'll be playing in** — its premise, tone, and surface situation, never the hidden truths — as the capstone of worldbuilding, and calls the `task_complete` tool in that same message. That overview is the last thing the player sees before character creation, so it must actually be there in the closing message, not left a few turns back. **Do not ask whether they're ready for character creation and do not announce the transition** — character creation opens on its own right after. Don't call `task_complete` before the overview has been given, or while you still intend to ask the player anything.
