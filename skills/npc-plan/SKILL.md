# NPC Staging

Stage the NPCs the session will feature so the runner plays them as **independent agents** — acting
on their own nature and knowledge, reacting to each other and the scene, not waiting on the PC.

## For each NPC in the scene

- **Disposition coming in** — how they regard the PC and the situation right now, and why.
- **Agenda** — what they want from this scene; what they'll push for, concede, or refuse.
- **Knows / doesn't** — what's in their `Known to:` set; they act only on what they actually know.
  Flag anything secret.
- **Voice anchor** — a one-line personality/speech handle so the runner keeps them consistent.
- **Reactions** — how they'll respond to the likely beats, *including to other NPCs*. A savvy ally
  reads a newcomer the oblivious PC trusts; a rival presses an opening. Plan these so the runner
  doesn't funnel every decision back to the PC.

## Concealed tells

When an NPC is hiding a background, motive, or deception, design the **tell** as a check-gated
reveal (the general gating mechanic is in `investigation-plan`), with an NPC-specific twist:

- **List the specific perceivable signs** — a word chosen too carefully, familiarity with a place
  they claim not to know, a glance at the sealed door.
- **Gate them behind an Insight check the *player* initiates.** Unlike most checks, the runner does
  **not** offer or prompt it — offering it is itself a signal to a PC who'd never think to
  scrutinize. The signs are **not narrated for free**; a trusting or oblivious PC won't register
  them unless the player chooses to scrutinize and rolls.
- **Name who'd catch it unaided.** Mark which present NPCs are sharp enough to notice on their own,
  so the tension plays out between *them* and the concealer in-fiction — not by telegraphing the
  secret to the player.

## Principles

- **Cross-reference against canon.** Every disposition, agenda, secret, and `Known to:` set must
  match the world/NPC/faction files, active arcs, and recorded documents. Don't contradict an
  established relationship or hand an NPC knowledge they were never given; carry forward how the PC
  last left them (per the ledger and last session).
- **NPCs are not satellites of the PC.** They perceive, judge, and act on their own. Once the PC
  commits to a course, the NPC who'd take charge should act rather than wait to be consulted.
- **Not uniformly kind.** Friction, suspicion, agendas. Kindness is a choice, not the default.
- **Consistency with what they know.** An NPC never acts on a secret they aren't party to.
