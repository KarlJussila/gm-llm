A new campaign is being created. Load `campaign-setup` for the authoring standards and principles that apply throughout init. Then load `campaign-intake` and follow its task list to gather the campaign brief from the player. When intake is complete, load `world-build` and `canon-conventions` and follow their task lists to build the world skeleton and write `campaign/campaign.md`.

When the world skeleton is built, finish the stage: call the `task_complete` tool and **pass a spoiler-free overview of the world in its `player_message` argument** — the premise, tone, and surface situation the player will begin in, never the hidden truths, written for them. That argument is the **only** way the overview reaches the player: a closing message you type as prose is **not** surfaced to them, so the overview has to go in the tool call itself. Don't present it and wait for approval — calling the tool ends the stage. Don't call `task_complete` before the world is genuinely built.
